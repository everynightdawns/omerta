Omerta is an experimental 5-piece band, combining myriad genres from rap and hyperpop to glitchcore, bossa nova, and more, then recontextualizing them within the framework of metal and rock. Comprised of members from various cultural and ethnic backgrounds, we express ourselves and our identities through an amalgamation of equally diverse visual and sonic styles. We wear these influences and inspirations on our sleeve in an effort to challenge the artistic and cultural status quo.

Taking notes from the genre-mashing trailblazers before us, we also recognize the greater impact of the legacies of bands like System of a Down, Linkin Park, and others. Because of the unapologetic integration of their heritage into their work, they inspired an entire generation of youth, including ourselves, and carved out a space for the perennial "other". This, too, is our raison d'être.

The following playlist is a collection of a few preproduction demos off our upcoming EP "Suicycle". It is intended to be a companion album to our debut EP "Hyperviolence". "Suicycle" expresses themes of discontentment, disillusionment, and disaffection; however, unlike its predecessor, the effects of these are primarily directed internally rather than externally.

We parallel this sentiment through the songs themselves — still incorporating elements from a variety of music, but the palette is more unhinged and unpredictable. On one level, this is done symbolically to convey the aforementioned themes with regard to the post-modern attention economy. Unrepentantly dissident, we reject the comforts reserved for those willing to forsake their individuality in exchange for a veneer of agreeability. On another level, this is the opening salvo to our war on the sonic stagnancy and strict adherence to uninspired tropes that seek to undermine everything we, and many others, love about music. We must first destroy before we can build anew.
